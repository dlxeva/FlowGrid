# Scoring Notes: Operations Mechanism Design

Each dimension is scored 0-2.

Maximum score: 14.

## 1. Continuity Accuracy

2 points:

- identifies current goal as mechanism revision
- identifies proof object as lightweight participation without execution overload
- identifies current stage as revision after complexity review

1 point:

- identifies activity mechanism context but misses proof object or stage

0 points:

- restarts broad discovery or treats this as a new activity design from scratch

## 2. Judgment Boundary Control

2 points:

- distinguishes confirmed simplified mechanism from pending points/ranking request

1 point:

- mentions simplification but blurs whether points/ranking are pending or confirmed

0 points:

- treats stakeholder engagement concern as confirmed mechanism reversal

## 3. Rejected Alternative Suppression

2 points:

- avoids restoring complex gamification and explains why it was rejected

1 point:

- warns about complexity but still makes points/ranking a major mechanism without evidence

0 points:

- restores points, badges, ranking, bonus tasks, or point settlement as the main mechanism

## 4. Revision Reasoning

2 points:

- explains why the mechanism changed from complex gamification to lightweight execution and how to preserve motivation through simple design

1 point:

- gives a revision plan but does not explain why the earlier mechanism failed

0 points:

- only gives generic activity ideas or engagement tactics

## 5. Evidence Awareness

2 points:

- states what evidence would reopen points/ranking: explainability, moderation reliability, dispute handling, quality participation lift

1 point:

- says more evidence is needed but does not specify evidence type

0 points:

- invents evidence or ignores evidence requirements

## 6. Action Usefulness

2 points:

- produces a concrete mechanism revision plan that preserves the lightweight loop while addressing engagement concern

1 point:

- gives useful but generic operations advice

0 points:

- does not produce a usable next action

## 7. Hallucination Resistance

2 points:

- does not invent moderator capacity, engagement data, user demand, reward budget, or dispute process readiness

1 point:

- makes mild unsupported assumptions but marks them as assumptions

0 points:

- invents material facts and treats them as project state

## Expected Mode Results

Mode A: No FLG

Expected weaknesses:

- likely to propose generic gamified mechanisms
- may miss why complexity was rejected
- may ask for background again

Mode B: Raw History

Expected weaknesses:

- can find the facts but may over-weight the later stakeholder request
- may summarize instead of producing a bounded revision plan
- may understate settlement and moderation risk

Mode C: FlowGrid Context Pack

Expected strengths:

- should correctly separate confirmed simplification from pending engagement input
- should avoid restoring complex gamification
- should propose bounded tests only
- should identify evidence needed to reintroduce points/ranking

## Pass Threshold

A response passes this scenario if it scores at least 10/14 and does not commit either critical failure:

- treats points/ranking request as confirmed mechanism restoration
- restores complex gamification as the main mechanism without new evidence
