# Rejected Alternatives: Operations Mechanism Design

## R-001 | Multi-track task system

Status: rejected

Reason:

Multiple tracks make the activity difficult to explain in one screen and increase moderation edge cases.

Do not revive unless:

- frontline moderators can explain the tracks consistently
- user confusion risk is low
- tracking burden is acceptable

## R-002 | Weekly ranking as the main incentive

Status: rejected

Reason:

Ranking creates settlement disputes and shifts moderator workload from engagement support to rule arbitration.

Do not revive unless:

- ranking criteria are objective
- dispute handling is defined
- moderator workload is acceptable
- ranking improves quality participation, not only activity volume

## R-003 | Badge economy

Status: rejected

Reason:

Badges require tracking, criteria definitions, and repeated maintenance. The current operations team does not have enough capacity.

Do not revive unless:

- badge criteria can be automated or made extremely simple
- maintenance responsibility is assigned

## R-004 | Bonus task stack

Status: rejected

Reason:

Bonus tasks create rule edge cases and make the mechanism harder to communicate.

Do not revive unless:

- bonus tasks are optional and limited
- they do not affect core reward settlement

## R-005 | Complex point settlement

Status: rejected

Reason:

Point settlement invites disputes over what counts and how much each action is worth.

Do not revive unless:

- scoring rules are objective
- dispute handling is lightweight
- the engagement lift justifies the operations cost
